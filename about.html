From chaos to systems.

Hey there, I'm Nurul. Let me share my journey with you

My love for technology began when I was in high school. Not just for fun, but to solve problems

In high school, I spent my time volunteering to teach children outside of school. Every child had different needs—some needed help with math homework, others needed new books or backpacks. We kept track manually, but it quickly became chaotic. Notes got lost, requests piled up, and we struggled to organize who needed what and who could help.

So, I built a solution: a volunteer coordination app. Instead of scattered notes, we had a system where volunteers could log each child’s needs and mark which ones they could fulfill. Suddenly, our efforts were more organized, efficient, and impactful—we could help more kids, faster, with less confusion.

Then in college, I took on something even bigger: leading a coin collection project for charity. What started as a small initiative to gather spare change for scholarships quickly turned into an organizational nightmare. Friends kept calling, eager to donate, but coordinating pickups became overwhelming.

So, I built another solution: a coin pickup scheduling app. Donors could schedule a time, and volunteers could be assigned pickups, making the entire process seamless. The result? More donations, less hassle, and a scalable system that worked effortlessly. Our efforts paid off—we successfully funded scholarships for three students for an entire semester.

That’s when I realized: Technology is my superpower for solving real-world inefficiencies.

I took that mindset into my tech career, working as a frontend engineer, building scalable web applications. I developed real-time apps, interactive platforms, and automation tools—always with the goal of making processes smoother and more intuitive. Whether it was helping a nursing home streamline communication, building a job board for better hiring transparency, or creating tools to enhance digital accessibility, my focus remained the same:

Turn chaos into systems. Use tech to empower people. Make an impact.

Now, I bring that same mindset to solving real-world inefficiencies. Where is your organization struggling? What repetitive, frustrating process is slowing you down? Let’s fix it—with technology that works for you, not against you.
